# Charter of the United Interstellar Council

*Established by the Republic of India on behalf of the founding member agencies*

---

## Preamble

In recognition of the shared challenges facing all of humanity among the stars, and in the spirit of cooperation transcending prior political divisions, embargoes, and conflicts, the undersigned agencies do hereby establish the United Interstellar Council as a forum for collective governance, mutual accord, and the advancement of common purpose.

---

## Article I — Membership

### Section 1 — Eligibility
All agencies, regardless of prior political history, embargo stance, or national affiliation, are eligible to apply for membership in the Council.

### Section 2 — Admission
Admission of a new member requires a simple majority vote (more than 50%) of all current member agencies. A quorum must be present for the vote to be valid.

### Section 3 — Voluntary Withdrawal
Any member agency may withdraw from the Council at any time by formal declaration. Withdrawal takes effect immediately. A withdrawing agency forfeits all voting rights and council privileges upon departure.

### Section 4 — Expulsion
A member agency may be expelled from the Council by a supermajority vote of two-thirds (2/3) of all current member agencies. A quorum must be present. The agency facing expulsion shall be permitted to address the Council before the vote is held, but shall not vote on the matter of their own expulsion.

### Section 5 — Reinstatement
An expelled agency may reapply for membership under the standard admission process defined in Section 2. No minimum waiting period is imposed.

---

## Article II — Equality of Members

### Section 1 — Voting Rights
Each member agency holds exactly one (1) vote on all matters brought before the Council. No agency shall hold veto power, exemption power, or weighted voting privilege. All member agencies are equal in standing.

### Section 2 — Proposal Rights
Any member agency in good standing may submit proposals for the Council's consideration. There is no limit on the number of proposals a member may submit.

---

## Article III — Quorum

### Section 1 — Requirement
A quorum of more than fifty percent (50%) of all current member agencies must be present for any vote to be valid. No binding decision may be taken in the absence of a quorum.

### Section 2 — Determination
Quorum is determined at the time a vote is called. If quorum is lost during proceedings, no further votes may be held until quorum is restored.

---

## Article IV — Chairman

### Section 1 — Role and Responsibilities
The Chairman of the Council shall:
1. Set the order of proposals on the Council agenda.
2. Preside over Council proceedings and maintain order.
3. Cast the deciding vote in the event of a tie.

4. Emergency suspend any active vote, halting proceedings immediately. All votes cast up to the point of suspension are recorded, and agencies that had not yet voted are recorded as "did not vote."

The Chairman holds no additional powers beyond those enumerated above. The Chairman's regular vote is counted as any other member's vote; the tie-breaking power is exercised only when a vote results in an exact tie. The emergency suspension power is intended for extraordinary circumstances and its use is recorded in the Council record.

### Section 2 — Election
The Chairman is elected by a simple majority vote (more than 50%) of all member agencies present, provided a quorum is met. Any member agency may nominate itself or another member agency for the position.

### Section 3 — Term of Office
The Chairman serves until a new election is called and concluded. There is no fixed term limit.

### Section 4 — Calling an Election
Any member agency may call for a new Chairman election once per standard year. The call must be seconded by at least one other member agency. Upon receiving a valid call and second, a vote on whether to hold an election is conducted. If more than fifty percent (50%) of members present vote in favour (with quorum met), a new election is held immediately.

### Section 5 — Vacancy
If the Chairman's agency is expelled, withdraws, or is otherwise unable to serve, a new election shall be held at the next Council session. In the interim, agenda-setting duties fall to the longest-serving member agency.

---

## Article V — Voting Thresholds

### Section 1 — Standard Decisions
Agreements, initiatives, laws, and all ordinary Council business require a simple majority vote (more than 50%) of member agencies present, provided a quorum is met.

### Section 2 — Expulsion
Expulsion of a member agency requires a supermajority vote of two-thirds (2/3) of all current member agencies.

### Section 3 — Charter Amendments
Any amendment to this Charter requires a supermajority vote of two-thirds (2/3) of all current member agencies. The proposed amendment must be submitted to all members at least one full session before the vote.

---

## Article VI — Enforcement

### Section 1 — Compliance
Member agencies are expected to honour and uphold all active agreements, initiatives, and laws passed by the Council. Non-compliance may be raised by any member as a matter of Council business.

### Section 2 — Sanctions
The Council may, by simple majority vote, impose sanctions on a non-compliant member. Sanctions may include but are not limited to:
1. Formal censure and public record of non-compliance.
2. Suspension of the agency's voting rights for a defined period.
3. Suspension of Council privileges and protections.

### Section 3 — Escalation
Repeated or severe non-compliance may be grounds for expulsion proceedings under Article I, Section 4.

---

## Article VII — Amendments

### Section 1 — Proposal
Any member agency may propose an amendment to this Charter. The proposed text must be distributed to all member agencies at least one full Council session prior to the vote.

### Section 2 — Ratification
Amendments require a supermajority vote of two-thirds (2/3) of all current member agencies to be ratified. A quorum must be present.

### Section 3 — Entry into Force
Ratified amendments take effect immediately upon passage unless the amendment itself specifies otherwise.

---

*So ratified by the founding members of the United Interstellar Council.*
