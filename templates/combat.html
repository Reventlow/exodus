{# === ROUND STRUCTURE ============================================== #}
■

ROUND STRUCTURE

▸ INITIATIVE  — roll once at scene start. Pool = Dexterity + Composure. Highest acts first; ties resolve by Composure, then Dexterity. Surprise bypasses Defense for the entire first round if the target was unaware.

▸ TURN  — on your initiative you may take one action (an attack, a discipline of magic, a complex maneuver) plus any number of free actions (talking, dropping prone, dropping a weapon). Reflexive defense and dodge do not consume the turn. Drawing or switching a weapon mid-fight is an action — see ACTION COSTS below.

▸ END OF ROUND  — resolve ongoing effects (bleeding, fire, poison), tick durations down, repeat from the top.

READY GATE

During the setup phase, every Character participant must toggle READY on their row before the GM can roll initiative and start combat. The toggle confirms the player has chosen their starting layout — equipped weapons, equipped armor, taken cover, applied any starting conditions like prone, etc.

The GM can FORCE START to bypass the gate when a player is offline or unresponsive. The override is a superuser-only checkbox surfaced next to the START button; it appears only while at least one Character is unready.

▸ NPCs and mooks don't ready up  — they're GM-controlled and bypass the gate. Only player-character rows count toward the READY: N / M CHARACTERS tally.

▸ Setup-only flag  — once combat is active, the READY tag is automatically stripped from every participant. Rewinding via CLEAR INITIATIVE does NOT auto-re-ready; players opt back in. A fresh ready_change log row fires on every toggle and broadcasts over WebSocket so the table sees state shifts in real time.

{# === DEFENSE & DAMAGE ============================================= #}
■

DEFENSE & DAMAGE

▸ DEFENSE  — passive. Subtract lower of Dexterity or Wits + Athletics from the attacker's pool. Applies to melee and thrown attacks. Does not apply to firearms or to the first round of a surprise attack.

▸ DODGE  — active alternative to Defense. Roll Defense as a dice pool; each success cancels one of the attacker's. Burns the turn's action.

▸ ARMOR  — subtracts directly from the attacker's net successes. Two values: bashing/lethal (e.g., kevlar 1/2 · plate 2/3 · powered armor 3/3 + Pain Tolerance).

▸ DAMAGE  — net successes after Defense and armor are applied to the health track. Three types in order of severity: bashing · lethal · aggravated. Bashing fills the track from the left; lethal and aggravated upgrade existing wounds and override.

{# === COVER & CONCEALMENT ========================================== #}
■

COVER & CONCEALMENT

Cover stops bullets; concealment stops sight. They stack — a target in dense smoke behind a wall benefits from both.

COVER TIERS

TIER PENALTY EXAMPLES NOTES
LIGHT −2 Door frame, chair, low fence, parked car (side) ≤ 50% body shielded
HEAVY −4 Sandbag, vehicle hood, brick wall corner, dumpster ≥ 75% body shielded
FULL cannot target Behind solid wall, sealed bulkhead, building corner Target must expose to be attacked. AOE / explosives bypass.
CONCEAL. −1 / −2 / −3 Smoke, dim light, fog, rain, thermal drape Affects sight only. Bullets pass cleanly.

USING COVER

▸ ENTERING  — moving up to Speed metres into cover is part of your turn's action; once behind it, the cover penalty applies to incoming fire from the next attacker forward.

▸ POP-UP FIRE  — popping out to attack is standard, but you become attackable that turn at half cover bonus (light → −1, heavy → −2) until your next initiative.

▸ BLIND FIRE  — firing without exposing yourself: keep full cover bonus, but your own attack pool takes −2 (no aim, no sight).

▸ MOVING BETWEEN COVER  — a 2 m+ dash exposes you for the run. Defenders at half Defense until your next initiative; suppressing fire denies the move.

▸ AUTOFIRE / SUPPRESSION  — sustained fire on a cover position forces anyone behind it to stay down (no pop-up actions) for the duration. Ammo cost: full burst (10+ rounds) per turn suppressed.

DESTROYING COVER

Cover has Durability and Health. Damage that exceeds Durability eats Health; when Health hits 0 the cover collapses and the bonus is gone. Live from /settings/ → COMBAT → Cover.

{# === HEALTH TRACK & CONSEQUENCES =================================== #}
■

HEALTH & CONSEQUENCES

Total boxes = Stamina + Size (typically 7 for a Size 5 human with Stamina 2). The rightmost three boxes carry −1 / −2 / −3 wound penalties (left → right). The penalty applies to every dice pool — attacks, defense, dodge, skill rolls, everything. See the dedicated subsection below for worked examples.

▸ INCAPACITATED  — full track of bashing → unconscious for [Stamina] minutes. Full track of lethal → unconscious + bleeding 1L per minute until stabilized. Full track of aggravated → dead.

▸ HEALING  — bashing heals 1 box per 15 minutes rest. Lethal heals 1 box per 2 days (less in field conditions). Aggravated heals 1 box per week. Medical care doubles bashing and lethal rates; cybernetic limbs heal as integrity damage on the limb itself.

▸ HEAL / ADJUST HP (GM)  — v0.15.26 adds a GM-only HP (GM ADJUST) sub-form on every participant row. Three integer fields (B / L / A) are clamped to 0..health_max; the cumulative sum is also bounded by health_max. The incapacitated condition tag is auto-toggled based on the new total. Logged as health_adjust with before / after snapshots so the timeline shows narrative healing alongside attack damage.

WOUND PENALTIES

▸ THE RULE  — the rightmost three boxes of the health track carry −1 / −2 / −3 penalties (left → right). The penalty kicks in the moment damage reaches that box. Applies to every dice pool — attack, defense, dodge, skill rolls, willpower rolls.

▸ FORMULA  — penalty = max(0, filled − (max − 3)) as a positive number; treated as a negative dice modifier server-side.

▸ WORKED EXAMPLE (7-BOX TRACK)

DAMAGE TRACK PENALTY
0–4⬜ in penalty zone0
53rd-from-end filled−1
62nd-from-end filled−2
7last filled (incapacitated)−3

▸ COMBINES WITH  — condition modifiers (prone, stunned, blinded, grappled). The participant row's MOD chip shows the combined wound + condition total at a glance; the row's WP chip shows wound alone.

▸ MERITS  — auto-detected as of v0.15.22. Increased Pain Threshold reduces the wound penalty by 1 toward zero (−1 → 0, −2 → −1, −3 → −2); Pain Tolerance short-circuits the penalty to 0 regardless of damage taken (and overrides Increased Pain Threshold when both are present). The participant row's WP chip and the attack pool breakdown reflect the reduced value automatically. Detection reads the canonical merit_entries M2M plus the legacy merits_old JSONField — case-insensitive name match. Berserker, Concentration, and Daredevil remain GM-applied via the GM modifier input on the attack form (their trigger conditions aren't derivable from game state).

SKILL-DICE MERITS — MARTIAL ARTS, FENCING

▸ AUTO-APPLIED  — no checkbox, no input. The server detects the merit on the actor's sheet and adds +rating dice to the attack pool when the attack's resolved skill matches. If the attack uses a different skill, the merit doesn't fire — the bonus is targeted, not generic.

▸ MARTIAL ARTS → BRAWL  — +rating dice on every attack resolved with the Brawl skill: unarmed strikes, grapples, improvised-by-fist attacks, and any weapon whose category resolves to Brawl.

▸ FENCING → WEAPONRY  — +rating dice on every attack resolved with the Weaponry skill: melee weapons (knives, batons, swords, hammers, axes, etc.) and improvised weapons that auto-pick to Weaponry.

▸ STACKS WITH  — the underlying skill rating, the weapon's dice modifier, any matching specialisations, willpower spend, and aim. Wound and condition penalties still subtract afterwards. Off-hand attacks honour the bonus too — a Fencing duellist with a knife in each hand applies the bonus to both rolls.

▸ DETECTION  — canonical Character.character_merits (or NPC.npc_merits) M2M to MeritDefinition, case-insensitive name match, plus the through-row's rating field. Mooks have no merits and never receive the bonus. The legacy merits_old JSONField has no rating field and is intentionally NOT consulted — re-attach the merit canonically to drive the bonus.

▸ NON-ATTACK ROLLS  — dodge and initiative do not route through the attack pool helper, so neither merit fires on those rolls. Future skill-roll surfaces (e.g. melee parry rolls) will need explicit wiring if they want the bonus.

▸ TIMELINE + UI  — the attack form's breakdown line shows + MARTIAL ARTS N / + FENCING N after WEAPON when the bonus fires. The attack log payload carries skill_merit_name (str or null) and skill_merit_bonus (int) on every row, with a (MARTIAL ARTS +N) / (FENCING +N) message tail when bonus > 0.

AIMING

▸ COST  — spends your full turn against a specific named target. The pointer does not auto-advance; the GM steps the round forward when ready.

▸ BENEFIT  — grants +1 dice on your next attack against that target. Stackable up to +3 over consecutive aim turns (re-aiming the same target each turn).

▸ BREAKS ON  — taking damage · taking full defense · dodging · attacking a different target · the round ending. Aim does not carry between rounds.

▸ SWITCHING TARGETS  — resets the bonus to +1 (does not stack across targets).

BURST FIRE

Available only on auto-capable firearms (catalogue flag: Sub Machine Gun, Assault Rifle, Auto Shotgun in the default seed). Single-shot pistols, DMRs, pump shotguns and bolt-action rifles stay single-fire even on a burst-mode POST — the server silently downgrades and writes a system-log row.

MODE ROUNDS DICE NOTES
SINGLE 1 +0 Default for every firearm. No spread.
SHORT BURST 3 +1 Single target only — no spread.
MEDIUM BURST 10 +2 Up to 3 targets total (1 primary + 2 extras).
LONG BURST / FULL AUTO 20 +3 Up to 6 targets total (1 primary + 5 extras).

X-AGAIN — WEAPON-SPECIFIC EXPLODE THRESHOLDS

▸ 10-AGAIN (DEFAULT)  — a rolled 10 succeeds AND re-rolls. The re-roll counts as another success on 8+ and itself re-rolls on 10. Default for every catalogue entry as of v0.15.19.

▸ 9-AGAIN  — explodes on 9 or 10. Re-rolls feed back into the same threshold (a 9 on the explode chain re-rolls just like a 10 does in 10-again).

▸ 8-AGAIN  — explodes on 8, 9, or 10. The most generous tier — every success can chain another roll.

▸ SUCCESS THRESHOLD UNCHANGED  — 8+ is one success at every tier. The X-again number is the explosion trigger, not a different success threshold. A 9-again attack still counts an 8 as one success; a 9 still counts as one success and now also explodes.

▸ CHAIN CAP  — the explosion chain is capped at 5 levels per starting die. A pathological all-trigger streak will halt at five re-rolls regardless of tier.

▸ CONFIGURATION  — per weapon at /settings/ → COMBAT → Weapons → AGAIN. Default is 10. Catalogue edits do not retroactively mutate equipped weapons — the participant's snapshot wins until they re-equip.

▸ ATTACK FORM BADGE  — a 9-AGAIN or 8-AGAIN badge appears next to the equipped weapon name on the participant row when the snapshot's threshold is non-default. Both main-hand and off-hand are badged independently.

▸ DICE TIMELINE  — every triggering face lights up with the same bold-glow treatment 10s currently use, so a 9-again 9 reads exactly like a classic 10-again 10. A 9 in a 10-again attack stays a plain success.

▸ DEFAULT CATALOGUE  — as of v0.15.19, no default weapon ships with anything other than 10-again. The GM tunes individual entries per table.

AUTOFIRE SPREAD

▸ ELIGIBILITY  — medium and long bursts only. Single and short bursts cannot spread.

▸ TARGET CAPS  — medium burst engages up to 3 targets total (1 primary + 2 extras); long burst up to 6 targets total (1 primary + 5 extras).

▸ ROLLS  — each spread target rolls a separate attack, with its own defense, cover, and armor mitigation.

▸ PENALTY  — cumulative −1 dice per extra target. First extra: −1; second extra: −2; third: −3; and so on.

▸ AIM  — the aim bonus only applies to the primary target. Extras get the burst bonus but not the aim bonus.

AMMO & RELOAD

▸ MAGAZINE  — each catalogue firearm carries a magazine size in rounds (Hand Gun 15 · Large Hand Gun 8 · SMG 30 · Assault Rifle 30 · DMR 10 · Shotgun 6 · Twin-Barrel 2 · Auto Shotgun 8 · Scoped Rifle 5 · Taser cartridge 1).

▸ START STATE  — participants start combat with a full magazine (set on equip and on combat start).

▸ CONSUMPTION  — single shot = 1 round · short burst = 3 · medium = 10 · long = 20.

▸ INSUFFICIENT AMMO  — if the magazine has fewer rounds than the requested burst mode needs, the server silently downgrades to SINGLE and logs a system warning.

▸ EMPTY MAGAZINE  — attempting to fire with zero rounds is rejected with an OUT OF AMMO flash; you must reload first.

▸ RELOAD  — resets the magazine to capacity. Always available — players have unlimited magazines (no reserve tracking).

▸ RELOAD COST  — costs your full turn when called by the active participant. Off-turn reloads (GM bookkeeping) are free but flagged in the log as out-of-band.

▸ RELOAD OFF-HAND (v0.15.26)  — RELOAD OFF-HAND (current/max) button now sits next to the main RELOAD on the STANCE strip whenever an off-hand firearm is equipped with magazine > 0. Same cost model as the main-hand reload; the parallel offhand_ammo:N tag is reset independently. Closes the deferred item from v0.15.16.

SURPRISE ROUND

▸ TOGGLE  — GM-only checkbox next to the START ENCOUNTER button: SURPRISE ROUND (defenders without "Alert" merit get no defense in round 1). Stored on Encounter.metadata["is_surprise_round"]; no schema change.

▸ EFFECT  — round 1 only. Every defender lacking the per-row Alert exemption returns defense = 0 regardless of all other modifiers (trumps Dex+Athletics, FULL DEFENSE doubling, dodge successes, condition mods). Round 2+ falls back to normal defense computation.

▸ ALERT EXEMPTION  — per-participant flag (Participant.surprise_immune). Surfaces on the row as a cyan [ALERT] chip. The GM toggles via the TOGGLE ALERT sub-form in the action panel. Used for characters / NPCs with the Alert merit (or its NPC equivalent).

▸ TIMELINE  — attack rows landing on a surprised target carry surprised: true on the payload and append (SURPRISE — no defense) to the message tail.

ALL-OUT ATTACK

▸ COST / BENEFIT  — checkbox on the attack form: +2 dice on the primary attack, but the attacker forfeits Defense for the round.

▸ DURATION  — the all_out_attack condition tag (def_mod = −99, effectively zero defense) sticks on the attacker until the round boundary. Cleared automatically alongside defense_full / dodging:N / aiming_at:* in the round-roll housekeeping.

▸ MUTEX WITH FULL DEFENSE  — rejected server-side if the attacker already has defense_full set this round. The two stances are logically incompatible (you can't both forfeit and double your defense in one round).

▸ SCOPE  — bonus applies to the primary attack only. Spread extras and off-hand resolutions don't double-dip on the +2.

▸ TIMELINE  — attack rows carry all_out: true and all_out_bonus: 2 on the payload; message tail appends (ALL-OUT). The LIVE TOTAL preview reflects the +2 the moment the checkbox is ticked.

KNOCKDOWN

▸ TRIGGER  — a successful hit with a weapon whose catalogue entry has the knockdown_capable flag set. Default seed: Shotgun, Twin-Barrel Shotgun, Auto Shotgun. The KO column in /settings/ → COMBAT → Weapons opts other firearms in.

▸ AUTO-FIRES  — no checkbox on the attack form. The resolver triggers it automatically on every successful hit by a knockdown-capable weapon. Skipped when the target is already prone (you can't knock down someone on the ground).

▸ CONTEST  — the target rolls Strength + Stamina (clean WoD 2.0 contest — no GM modifier, no wound penalty) against the attacker's success count. Mooks borrow mook_combat_pool / 3 as a stand-in resistance pool.

▸ OUTCOME  — resistance < attacker successes ⇒ target gains the prone tag. Resistance ≥ attacker successes ⇒ target stays up. Both branches log a knockdown CombatLog row with the resistance pool, dice, and result for narrative completeness.

▸ ROW BADGE  — a cyan KNOCKDOWN badge surfaces on the participant row whenever the equipped weapon (main or off-hand) carries the flag.

DUAL-WIELDING

▸ EQUIP OFF-HAND  — pick a second weapon via the EQUIP OFF-HAND sub-form on the participant action panel. The off-hand snapshot is independent of the main hand — catalogue edits don't retroactively mutate equipped off-hands.

▸ DUAL ATTACK  — tick the DUAL ATTACK checkbox on the attack form (only visible when an off-hand is equipped) to fire both weapons in one action. Both attacks resolve against the same primary target.

▸ MAIN HAND  — full pool with all bonuses: aim · burst · autofire spread · willpower +3 · specialisations · GM mod.

▸ OFF-HAND  — −2 dice penalty. No aim bonus (consumed by main hand) · no WP +3 (already spent) · no burst (always single-shot) · no autofire spread. Same defense / cover / armor resolution applies. Off-hand uses its own weapon's dice modifier and auto-picked skill (firearm → Firearms, melee → Weaponry, etc.).

▸ AMBIDEXTROUS MERIT  — Physical, 1 dot. Removes the −2 off-hand penalty. Detection looks at both the canonical merit list and any legacy free-text merit entry; the DUAL ATTACK label adapts to read AMBIDEXTROUS — no penalty when the merit is detected.

▸ AMMO  — off-hand ammo is tracked separately as offhand_ammo:N (parallel tag). The OFF-MAG indicator on the row reads independently of the main MAG.

▸ OFF-HAND OUT OF AMMO  — if the off-hand firearm is empty when DUAL ATTACK is requested, the main-hand attack still resolves and the off-hand resolution is skipped (logged as a system warning).

▸ OFF-HAND RELOAD (v0.15.26)  — closes the deferred item from v0.15.16. The RELOAD OFF-HAND (current/max) button on the STANCE strip resets the off-hand magazine to capacity. Same cost rules as the main-hand reload (turn cost on own turn; free off-turn for GM bookkeeping). Logged as reload_offhand.

GUN FU (SOLDIER ONLY)

▸ ELIGIBILITY  — soldier-only merit, 1 to 5 dots. Each dot grants 1 free auto-success per session in gun combat. Visible on the attack form only when the actor is a soldier with the merit, has remaining session uses, and the equipped weapon is a firearm.

▸ ACTIVATION  — enter an integer N in the GUN FU (+N SUCCESS) input on the attack form. Capped server-side at the remaining count. N=0 (default) declines the spend.

▸ DISTRIBUTION  — spread evenly across all targets in the action (primary + autofire spread extras + off-hand on dual-wield). The integer remainder lands on the first targets in declaration order, so the primary gets the bonus when the math doesn't divide cleanly.

▸ EXAMPLES  — Gun Fu 5 vs 4 targets (medium burst spread): 2 / 1 / 1 / 1 (primary gets the +1 remainder). Gun Fu 3 vs 2 targets (dual-wield, no spread): 2 / 1 (main-hand gets the +1 remainder). Gun Fu 5 vs 1 target (single shot): 5 (all uses on the one target). Gun Fu 7 vs 4 targets (long burst spread): 2 / 2 / 2 / 1.

▸ EFFECT  — bonus successes are added to the rolled success count after defense and cover are applied. They do not bypass full cover (a blocked shot stays blocked) and do persist even when the dice roll comes up empty (a 0-success roll plus 1 Gun Fu success = 1 success on damage math).

▸ NON-FIREARM  — ignored. The merit is gun combat only; submitting a spend on a melee / improvised / thrown / unarmed attack zeroes out server-side (silent — no flash, no rejection).

▸ PERSISTENCE  — spent uses are written to the underlying Character's merit_uses["Gun Fu"] immediately after the action. The character sheet's REMAINING display refreshes on next load. The GM resets per-session counters via the existing RESET MERIT USES action on the character sheet.

▸ TIMELINE  — each per-target attack row carries a gun_fu_bonus_successes integer payload key, plus a (GUN FU +N SUCC) message tail when bonus > 0. A summary system row records the total spend and remaining count after the action.
